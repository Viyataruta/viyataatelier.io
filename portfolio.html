The Value of User Experience

"User experience is the bridge between the user and the product, and design is the path that leads them there."

Project Overview

This project explored how UX design applies to different career paths by analyzing job postings, researching industry trends, and reflecting on key skills. Working collaboratively, my team and I investigated the essential UX competencies required for our dream roles, synthesized research findings, and presented our insights.

My Role: UX Design Product Manager
Skills Explored: UX research, product strategy, prototyping, stakeholder communication

Problem & Solution

The Problem:

User experience design is a critical component in various fields, but professionals require a diverse skill set to succeed. Understanding industry expectations and trends helps future UX professionals prepare for their careers.

The Solution:

Through job searches, skill categorization, and secondary research, our team identified the most in-demand UX skills and industry trends, allowing us to map out the necessary competencies for our career goals.

Process & Research

Job Search & Skill Analysis:

  • Researched job postings, selecting UX Design Product Manager as my target role
  • Identified key skills such as user research, prototyping, and cross-functional collaboration
  • Categorized required skills with my team to find common themes across UX-related roles

Industry Research:

  • Analyzed UX trends in product management, focusing on user-centered product development
  • Synthesized findings with my team to create a comprehensive view of UX's evolving role

Individual Contribution

  • Conducted research on the intersection of UX design and product management
  • Identified emerging UX trends shaping product development
  • Reflected on personal skill gaps and areas for growth in UX and product strategy

Outcome & Reflection

Final Outcome:

A team presentation and documented research outlining the value of UX across different fields, with insights into the skills required for a UX-focused career.

Learnings:

This project deepened my understanding of UX in product management and helped me identify key skills to develop further. It reinforced the importance of user research, stakeholder collaboration, and continuous learning in UX design.

Starship Robot Design

"Expanding Starship robots to deliver textbooks would streamline the process, offering students a fast, on-demand service that supports their academic needs."

Project Overview

Purdue's Starship robots currently deliver food, but what if they could do more? Our team explored how these autonomous robots could be used for textbook delivery, creating a convenient and efficient system for students. By reducing wait times and eliminating long bookstore lines, this service could streamline the textbook distribution process, enhancing accessibility and improving the student experience.

Audience: Purdue students, faculty, bookstore staff
Constraints: Limited Starship fleet, integration with bookstore inventory, user trust in automation

Problem & Solution

The Problem:

Students often face long lines and inconvenient pickup locations when retrieving textbooks, leading to delays and frustration.

The Solution:

Our team designed a Starship-powered textbook delivery system that allows students to order books and receive them at their dorm or a designated campus location, making the process faster and more accessible.

Process & Research

User Research:

  • Conducted interviews with students and bookstore staff to understand textbook pickup challenges
  • Identified key pain points, such as time constraints and difficulty accessing the bookstore during peak hours

Prototyping & Testing:

  • Designed a mobile interface allowing students to schedule textbook deliveries
  • Created a user journey outlining the order-to-delivery process
  • Iterated based on feedback, refining the system for usability and efficiency

Individual Contribution

  • Led user research by conducting interviews and analyzing survey data
  • Proposed an in-app scheduling feature based on student availability
  • Helped design a streamlined delivery request process for the bookstore

Outcome & Reflection

Final Outcome:

A concept prototype and user journey for textbook delivery using Starship robots, demonstrating how automation could improve access to academic resources.

Learnings:

This project taught me how to conduct user-centered research and design solutions that balance feasibility with innovation. It reinforced the importance of considering both technological capabilities and real user needs in UX design.
